# Geriatric Health Guidelines

## Age-Related Changes

### Normal Aging vs. Concerning Changes
- **Normal**: Mild forgetfulness (misplacing keys), slightly slower reflexes, needing reading glasses
- **Concerning**: Forgetting familiar routes, confusion about date/time, personality changes, sudden vision loss

### Nutrition for Elderly
- Eat smaller, more frequent meals (5-6 times a day)
- Include protein in every meal (dal, eggs, paneer, fish)
- Stay hydrated — minimum 6-8 glasses of water daily
- Calcium-rich foods: milk, ragi, green leafy vegetables
- Limit salt to reduce blood pressure risk
- Avoid excess sweets, especially with diabetes

### Exercise for Elderly
- Walk 20-30 minutes daily if able
- Gentle stretching exercises every morning
- Chair exercises for those with mobility issues
- Yoga and pranayama (breathing exercises) are excellent
- Never exercise on an empty stomach
- Stop immediately if feeling dizzy, chest pain, or shortness of breath

## Fall Prevention

### Home Safety
- Remove loose rugs and cables from walkways
- Install grab bars in bathroom near toilet and shower
- Ensure adequate lighting, especially at night (use night lights)
- Keep frequently used items at waist height
- Non-slip mats in bathroom and kitchen
- Wear proper footwear — avoid loose chappals

### Warning Signs of Fall Risk
- Difficulty getting up from a chair
- Unsteady walking or shuffling
- Dizziness when standing up
- Vision problems
- Previous falls in the last 6 months

## Sleep Health
- Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule
- Avoid heavy meals before bed
- Limit tea/coffee after 4 PM
- If unable to sleep, try warm milk or light reading
- Persistent insomnia may need medical evaluation

## Mental Health
- Stay socially connected — talk to family and friends regularly
- Engage in hobbies: reading, gardening, puzzles
- Loneliness and isolation increase health risks
- Signs of depression: persistent sadness, loss of interest, changes in appetite, sleep problems
- Depression in elderly is treatable — consult a doctor
