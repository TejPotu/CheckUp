# Medication Safety for Elderly

## Common Medications and Precautions

### Blood Pressure Medications
- **Amlodipine**: Take once daily. May cause ankle swelling — report if persistent.
- **Telmisartan**: Take at the same time each day. Avoid potassium-rich foods in excess.
- **Atenolol**: Do not stop suddenly. Check pulse before taking — if below 60 bpm, consult doctor.
- **General**: Stand up slowly to avoid dizziness. Monitor BP regularly.

### Diabetes Medications
- **Metformin**: Take with meals to reduce stomach upset. Do not skip meals after taking it.
- **Glimepiride**: Take before breakfast. Carries risk of low blood sugar — keep glucose tablets handy.
- **Insulin**: Store in refrigerator. Rotate injection sites. Never share needles.
- **General**: Regular blood sugar monitoring is essential. Watch for signs of low sugar: sweating, shakiness, confusion.

### Heart Medications
- **Aspirin (low-dose)**: Take with food. Report any unusual bleeding or bruising.
- **Atorvastatin**: Take at bedtime. Report any muscle pain or weakness.
- **Clopidogrel**: Do not stop without doctor's advice, especially after stent placement.

### Pain Medications
- **Paracetamol**: Safe for mild pain. Maximum 4 tablets (2g) per day. Avoid with liver problems.
- **Ibuprofen/Diclofenac**: Take with food. Avoid if kidney problems or stomach ulcers. Not for long-term use.
- **Tramadol**: May cause drowsiness. Avoid driving. Risk of dependency with long-term use.

## Drug Interaction Warnings
- Do NOT take multiple blood thinners without doctor's knowledge
- Antacids can reduce absorption of many medications — take 2 hours apart
- Grapefruit interacts with many heart and cholesterol medications
- Herbal supplements (ashwagandha, etc.) may interact with prescribed medicines — inform doctor

## Medication Adherence Tips
- Use a pill organizer to sort daily/weekly medicines
- Set phone alarms for medication times
- Keep an updated list of all medications, dosages, and timings
- Always carry medication list when visiting any doctor
- Never adjust dosage without consulting doctor
- If a dose is missed: take it as soon as remembered, unless it's almost time for the next dose. Never double up.

## When to Seek Immediate Help
- Severe allergic reaction: rash, swelling of face/throat, difficulty breathing
- Unexpected bleeding (blood in urine, stool, or vomiting blood)
- Sudden confusion or extreme drowsiness after taking medication
- Fainting or severe dizziness
