# Emergency Signs — When to Call for Help Immediately

> **If any of the following are reported, alert the caregiver immediately and advise calling 108.**

## Stroke Warning Signs (FAST Method)
- **F**ace: Is one side of the face drooping? Ask them to smile.
- **A**rms: Can they raise both arms? Does one drift downward?
- **S**peech: Is speech slurred or strange? Ask them to repeat a simple phrase.
- **T**ime: If any of these signs are present, call 108 immediately. Every minute counts.

### Additional Stroke Signs
- Sudden confusion or trouble understanding
- Sudden severe headache with no known cause
- Sudden trouble seeing in one or both eyes
- Sudden trouble walking, dizziness, loss of balance

## Heart Attack Signs
- Chest pain, pressure, or discomfort lasting more than a few minutes
- Pain or discomfort in arms, back, neck, jaw, or stomach
- Shortness of breath (with or without chest discomfort)
- Cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness
- **Women may have atypical symptoms**: unusual fatigue, indigestion-like feeling

## Breathing Emergencies
- Severe shortness of breath that's new or worsening
- Lips or fingertips turning blue/purple
- Gasping for breath or unable to speak in full sentences
- Choking on food — perform Heimlich maneuver if possible

## Fall-Related Emergencies
- Unable to get up after a fall
- Head injury — even if feeling okay, monitor for 24 hours
- Hip pain after fall — DO NOT try to walk, wait for help
- Visible bone deformity or inability to move a limb
- Loss of consciousness, even briefly

## Severe Allergic Reaction (Anaphylaxis)
- Swelling of face, lips, or throat
- Difficulty breathing or swallowing
- Hives or widespread rash
- Rapid heartbeat, dizziness, or fainting
- Use EpiPen if available, then call 108

## Other Emergencies
- **Severe bleeding** that doesn't stop with pressure
- **Sudden confusion** or disorientation (not normal for the person)
- **Seizures** — protect from injury, do not restrain, time the seizure
- **Severe abdominal pain** with vomiting
- **High fever** (above 103°F / 39.4°C) not responding to paracetamol
- **Diabetic emergency**: unconsciousness, severe confusion, or seizures related to blood sugar

## What To Do
1. Stay calm
2. Call emergency services: **108** (India ambulance)
3. Do NOT give food or water if the person is unconscious
4. Keep the person still and comfortable
5. If possible, note the time symptoms started
6. Have the medication list ready for paramedics
